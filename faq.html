FAQs

In this section we will cover frequently asked questions, safetly, and even recommend some gear for you!

What terrain will we skate on?

In all of our meetups we will be skating on smooth concrete or tarmac which are great for learning. You wont be expected to skate on anything gravely, go off road, or skate along a trail, so don't worry!

Can I come alone?

Yes! Everyone in the group is incredibly welcoming and we love to see newbies. We're a social bunch and very inviting, we can also help with teaching you some basics! get in touch with us here

Do I need to bring equipment?

If this is your fist time skating you are more than welcome to come along without skates or equipment, but we can't guarantee you will be able to skate. There will be plenty of people there to socialise with and get tips off if you are curious about skating and if someone has equipment they might let you use there's for a while, but always make sure to ask!

Safety

Safety is a major priority of the group, so please make sure to read this section carefully!

What equipment will I need?

We always recommend wearing a full set of safetly gear when you are learning to Skate. This includes kneepads, elbowpads, and a set of wrist guards. You can also wear a helmet if you choose. This items are designed to protect you when you fall and are mandatory for coming to the meetups, so make sure to pick yours up here.

Will I fall?

Falling is a part of skating that we all encounter, so what we'll show you during the meetups is how to reduce damage for when you do fall. For beginners, we always recommend going slow, skating with hands forward so you break you're fall, and staying close to other experienced skaters who will be able to help.

What if I injure myself?

This is something we will absolutely be trying to avoid through wearing protection and teaching proper technique. If you do become injured though, someone from the team will be able to provide first-aid and carry a box of medical supplies with us at all time. But keep in mind, this is not something we predict happening!

Equipment

There are plenty of options when it comes to equipment, so here are some of our recommendations to get you started!

Skates

There are a variety of skates to pick and choose from, and we have a couple of recommendations! Some of the best brand you can buy are Rookie, Ridel, and Rio. These are all trusted brands and great for first-timers! Of all of these though we would recommend Rookie the most, as they are very beginner friendly as the name suggests! You can browse and pick some up here. There is also a popular brand known as impala. We do not recommend these skates as they are designed with visual styling as a priority over pracricallity.

Accessories

There are a bunch of different accessories such as bags to carry you skates, straps to throw them over your shoulder, laces, stickers, different style toe-stops, you name it! None of these are essential but can make certain aspects like transporation easier or give you a look that suits your style! You can view some of these accessories here

Wheels

When you are starting out, the wheels that come with your skates should be more than enough to get you going. However, once you start getting a little more advanced these can be something to consider. We recommend when picking up a new set of wheels to go for something slightly wider than what will come with your skates, ideally over 60mm diameter. This will give you more control.
