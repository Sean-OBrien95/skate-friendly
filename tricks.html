Your Introduction to tricks!

In this section we'll be showing you some of the basic tricks to get you started! We know learning tricks can seem like an intimidating task when you're only starting out, which is why we've selected the below ones very carefully, all of these tricks have been designed with beginners in mind are great to try once you've found your balance on the skate!

The Bubble

The bubble is a great first trick to learn as it teaches you the basics of how your feet and legs are supposed to move in the skates. You want to start this move by having your feet point outwards ar a 90 degree angel. Once there, you want to push your legs outwards, and once you reach full extension, guide your feet back inwards, completing the bubble. You can try play around with this, and try doing it backwards like the below when you feel comfortable!

Transitions

Transitioning is a very useful technique and even one that most advanced skaters build off of when doing other tricks! As the name implies, this move will allow you to transistion from a front facing position to a back facing one. There are many ways to do this, but the best to learn first is the one shown in the video below is the two step. To do this, you will balance on one leg, turn your body outwards, and then lift the other foot and turn it around to meet the first one. We recommend trying this standing still before doing it with movement!

Skating Backwards

Once you've built up some confidence, it might be a good time to try going backwards! This is a great trick that always looks impressive, no matter what your level is. To do this, you'll have to get comfortable with the bubble trick shown above. Escencially, you'll want to do a backwards bubble continously to start, once you feel comforable doing this, you can start trying to do a bubble using only one foot at a time, like the video below. Then you'll alternate which foot you're doing the backwards bubble with, and voila! you're skating backwards! And don't worry if you don't get this straight away, this move can take some time!
